概覽

Taiwan's First Shared E-Scooter Platform — Campus-First Strategy

Led market growth for oloo — Taiwan's pioneering shared e-scooter service — operating in an unregulated environment with no regulatory playbook to follow. Inspired by Japan's Luup model, the strategy was to build a loyal user base first, then use that data to influence policy.

I developed partnerships across five university campuses, created experiential marketing initiatives, established educational programs around safety, and expanded operations to cultural districts in Kaohsiung.

機會與策略

Seven Opportunities & How I Addressed Them

01

Market Entry Strategy

How might we enter Taiwan's micromobility market before regulation exists?

策略

Adopted a Luup-inspired campus-first model to ensure legal compliance in an unregulated market — using controlled university environments as testing grounds before broader expansion.

02

Location Selection

How might we select initial locations that maximize impact while staying compliant?

策略

Selected five university campuses as initial deployment sites — controlled, high-footfall environments that allowed for rapid iteration, safety testing, and community relationship building.

03

User Acquisition

How might we drive first-time usage in a completely new product category?

策略

Experiential onboarding events with safety training, seasonal activation campaigns, and campus ambassador programs to drive organic word-of-mouth and first-ride conversions.

04

Brand Building

How might we build brand awareness and trust as Taiwan's first shared e-scooter?

策略

Established institutional credibility through formal university partnership agreements, co-branding opportunities, and safety certification programs — building trust before scale.

05

Geographic Expansion

How might we expand to tourism districts while solving government pain points?

策略

Developed government proposals for expanding into tourism districts in Kaohsiung — using campus data and user behavior insights to build the case for broader deployment.

06

Policy Advocacy

How might we accelerate regulatory development through market presence?

策略

Data-driven approach to influence regulatory development — presenting usage data, safety records, and community impact reports to local government stakeholders.

07

Marketing Strategy

How might we build a sustainable business model while operating in regulatory grey areas?

策略

Community leader partnerships for organic growth — working with student council leaders, campus influencers, and local community figures to drive authentic adoption.
